## raspberry pi 3 Model B REtro Console

Portable retro console with a 3.5-inch screen, powered by a Raspberry Pi 3 Model B and using the emulation software [Retropie](https://retropie.org.uk/). It does not have its own speakers, but you can connect normal headphones, and it uses two Super Nintendo USB controllers from [Amazon](https://www.amazon.es/gp/product/B073Z9MKKH/ref=ppx_yo_dt_b_search_asin_title?ie=UTF8&psc=1). It also has a 5000mAh portable battery, also from [Amazon](https://www.amazon.es/gp/product/B0825363Z4/ref=ppx_yo_dt_b_search_asin_title?ie=UTF8&psc=1), attached. Virtually all NES, SNES, Sega Master System, Sega Mega Drive, Sega Game Gear, Game Boy, Game Boy Color, Game Boy Advance, and many other older or lesser-known systems work, and even some PS1 and N64 games run well, such as Mario Kart or Star Wars Episode I: Racer. It is especially nice because you can remove the HDMI connector from the Raspberry Pi, connect it to any television or monitor with HDMI, and play on the big screen. It is ideal to carry in a backpack and play anywhere with a screen, or even without one, and it is a great way to catch up on classics you missed at the time. I ended up finishing Castlevania: Aria of Sorrow and Dragon Ball Advance Adventure for GBA.
